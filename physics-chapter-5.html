Chapter 5: Second Law of Thermodynamics

Study of direction of thermodynamic processes, heat engines, and entropy.

Thermodynamic Systems & Direction

The Second Law determines the direction of spontaneous thermodynamic processes and introduces the concept of entropy.

Spontaneous Process
Occurs naturally without external intervention
Irreversible Process
Cannot return to initial state without external work
Reversible Process
Can be reversed without leaving trace on surroundings

Second Law of Thermodynamics

Kelvin-Planck Statement

No heat engine can convert all absorbed heat into work

It is impossible to construct an engine which operates in a cycle and produces no other effect than the extraction of heat from a reservoir and the performance of an equivalent amount of work.

Clausius Statement

Heat cannot flow spontaneously from cold to hot

It is impossible to construct a refrigerator that operates in a cycle and produces no other effect than the transfer of heat from a cooler body to a hotter body.

Note: Both statements are equivalent. Violation of one implies violation of the other.
