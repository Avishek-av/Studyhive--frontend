Chapter 17: Organometallic Compounds

Compounds containing at least one direct bond between a carbon atom of an organic molecule and a metal.

Introduction to Organometallic Compounds

Definition: Organometallic compounds are chemical compounds containing at least one chemical bond between a carbon atom of an organic molecule and a metal.

General Formula
R-M

Where R = Organic group (alkyl, aryl, vinyl, etc.)

M = Metal atom (Li, Mg, Zn, Cu, etc.)

Nature of Carbon-Metal Bond:

  • The bond between carbon and metal is typically polar covalent
  • Carbon is more electronegative than most metals, making C-M bond polarized: CŒ¥--MŒ¥+
  • This polarization makes the carbon atom nucleophilic
  • Reactivity depends on the electronegativity difference between carbon and metal

R-Li (e.g., CH‚ÇÉLi - Methyllithium). Highly reactive, used in organic synthesis.
Organocopper Compounds
R-Cu (e.g., Gilman reagents). Used in coupling reactions.

Grignard Reagents
R-MgX. Most important organometallics in organic synthesis.

Key Characteristics:

  • Highly reactive toward electrophiles
  • Sensitive to air and moisture
  • Usually prepared and used under inert atmosphere
  • Excellent nucleophiles and strong bases
  • Widely used in C-C bond formation

Mechanism:

  1. Oxidative addition of R-X to Mg(0)
  2. Formation of radical intermediate
  3. Coordination with ether solvent
  4. Formation of stable Grignard reagent

  • Reaction must be protected from moisture and oxygen
  • Magnesium should be activated (freshly cleaned surface)
  • Reaction is exothermic - control temperature

Applications and Uses

Synthetic Applications:

C-C Bond Formation
Most important use - formation of new carbon-carbon bonds in organic synthesis.
Alcohol Synthesis
Preparation of primary, secondary, and tertiary alcohols from carbonyl compounds.
Carboxylic Acid Synthesis
From reaction with CO‚ÇÇ (important for chain elongation by one carbon).
Ketone Synthesis
From reaction with nitriles or acid chlorides.

Industrial Applications:

  • Pharmaceutical Industry: Synthesis of complex drug molecules
  • Agrochemicals: Production of pesticides and herbicides
  • Perfume Industry: Synthesis of fragrances and flavors
  • Polymer Industry: Preparation of monomers and specialty polymers
  • Research Laboratories: Versatile tool for organic synthesis

Other Organometallic Compounds:

Organolithium Compounds (R-Li):

  • More reactive than Grignard reagents
  • Prepared from alkyl halides and lithium metal
  • Used in lithium-halogen exchange reactions
  • Example: Butyllithium (n-BuLi)

  • Cannot be prepared from compounds with active hydrogen (-OH, -NH‚ÇÇ, -COOH, -SH)
  • Cannot be used with compounds containing carbonyl groups (except as intended)
  • Sensitive to air and moisture
  • Limited compatibility with some functional groups
