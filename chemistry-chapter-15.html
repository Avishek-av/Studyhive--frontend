  • Functional isomerism: With alkyl nitrites (R-O-N=O)

Higher than corresponding hydrocarbons due to polar nature of nitro group.

C‚ÇÜH‚ÇÖNO‚ÇÇ (Phenyl nitrate)

Introduction: Nitrobenzene is the simplest aromatic nitro compound. It is an important intermediate in the manufacture of aniline and other chemicals.

Physical Properties:

Appearance
Pale yellow oily liquid with characteristic odor of bitter almonds.
Boiling Point
210¬∞C. High boiling point due to polar nature and molecular association.
Density
1.20 g/cm¬≥. Heavier than water.
Toxicity
Highly toxic. Causes methemoglobinemia (blue baby syndrome).

Structure: The nitro group in nitrobenzene is planar and conjugated with the benzene ring, creating extensive resonance.

Resonance Effects:

  • -NO‚ÇÇ group is electron-withdrawing by both -I (inductive) and -M (mesomeric) effects
  • Deactivates benzene ring toward electrophilic substitution
  • Directs incoming electrophiles to meta-position
  • Stabilizes carbanion in nucleophilic substitution reactions

Uses of Nitrobenzene:

  • Manufacture of aniline (reduction product)
  • Production of benzidine (used in dye industry)
  • Solvent for cellulose ethers and esters
  • Manufacture of shoe polishes and floor polishes
  • Perfume industry (synthetic oil of mirbane)
