Transition Metals

d-block elements with incomplete d-orbitals. Known for variable oxidation states, colored compounds, and catalytic properties.

Introduction

Transition Metals

Elements with partially filled d-orbitals. Groups 3-12 of periodic table.

Examples: Fe, Cu, Zn, Cr, Mn, Ni, Co, Ag, Au, Pt.

Properties: High melting points, high density, good conductors.

Key Characteristics

  • Multiple oxidation states
  • Form colored compounds
  • Paramagnetic properties
  • Good catalysts
  • Form complex ions
  • High melting/boiling points

Oxidation States

Variable oxidation states due to similar energy of (n-1)d and ns orbitals.

  • Iron: +2, +3
  • Copper: +1, +2
  • Manganese: +2 to +7
  • Chromium: +3, +6

Higher oxidation states in oxides/fluorides.

Color & Catalysis

Color: Due to d-d electronic transitions.

Factors affecting color:

  • Nature of metal ion
  • Oxidation state
  • Type of ligands

Catalytic properties: Provide alternative reaction pathways.
